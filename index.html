The Rohlik Sales Forecasting Challenge on Kaggle tasked participants with predicting online grocery sales using historical data. The goal was to build time-series models to optimize inventory, pricing, and promotion strategies.

Rohlik Group, a leading European e-grocery innovator, operates across 11 warehouses in Czech Republic, Germany, Austria, Hungary, and Romania. This challenge focused on forecasting sales at each warehouse for the next 14 days.

I participated in this competition with my friends Venkatesh and Vishnu. Though we didn't rank near the top, we had a great time and learned a lot.

Our final model used LightGBM with features like date parts, lag features, and warehouse-specific patterns. A custom sales growth multiplier (1.05) improved forecast accuracy by accounting for long-term sales trends.

We also experimented with XGBoost and CatBoost, but LightGBM performed best. We believe blending all three might have improved our CV.
